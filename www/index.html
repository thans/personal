I am currently a student at The University of Washington, where I am studying Computer Engineering. Though I am continually fascinated by my Computer Science and Engineering courses, I am also very interested in communication and leadership. I excelled in my Public Speaking course and even took second in the UW Department of Communication's annual speech competition.

While at the University of Washington, I have had a number of chances to explore programming in different ways both in and outside of the classroom. Most recently, my two favorite courses were my Operating Systems and Computer Networks courses - I found the challenges presented in each of these courses as well as the engineering solutions we discussed (and the tradeoffs inherent in those solutions) very stimulating.

Outside of the classroom, I have learned through my time as a teaching assistant, my participation on the UW EcoCar 2 team, and the various projects I have done on the side. I was a teaching assistant at UW for introductory programming courses for 2 quarters and I plan to return for my final quarters at UW. On the UW EcoCar 2 team, I worked with a group of 4 other programmers and 3 designers to create an advanced touch screen interface for our vehicle. Some of the projects I have done on the side are available on my github account. Most recently, I worked on a Fantasy Football like game for "The Bachelor" and "The Bachelorette" with a friend of mine - make your team next time the show starts. Check out my projects page to learn more.
